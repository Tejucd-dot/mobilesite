Sunflower

üå± What is a Sunflower?

Sunflowers are large, bright yellow flowers that follow the sun's path across the sky. They're native to North America and can grow up to 12 feet tall.

ü¶ã What it Attracts

  • Bees (especially native bees)
  • Butterflies
  • Birds (goldfinches love the seeds)
  • Beneficial beetles

üåç Environmental Impact

  • Supports pollinators with abundant nectar
  • Produces oxygen and reduces CO2
  • Seeds feed wildlife through winter
  • Deep roots improve soil structure

üåø Plant Needs

  • Full sun (6-8 hours daily)
  • Well-drained soil
  • Regular watering
  • Protection from strong winds
  • Space to grow (3-6 feet apart)

Lavender

üå± What is Lavender?

Lavender is a fragrant herb with purple flower spikes. It's drought-tolerant, long-blooming, and beloved for its calming scent and beautiful appearance.

ü¶ã What it Attracts

  • Bees (honey bees and native bees)
  • Butterflies
  • Hummingbirds
  • Beneficial wasps

üåç Environmental Impact

  • Long blooming season supports pollinators
  • Drought-tolerant, conserves water
  • Natural pest deterrent
  • Air purification properties

üåø Plant Needs

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, alkaline soil
  • Minimal watering once established
  • Good air circulation
  • Annual pruning

Bee Balm

üå± What is Bee Balm?

Bee Balm is a native North American wildflower with tubular red, pink, or purple flowers. It's part of the mint family and has aromatic leaves.

ü¶ã What it Attracts

  • Bees (especially bumblebees)
  • Butterflies
  • Hummingbirds
  • Moths

üåç Environmental Impact

  • Native plant supporting local ecosystems
  • Attracts diverse pollinators
  • Natural antibacterial properties
  • Helps prevent soil erosion

üåø Plant Needs

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Regular watering
  • Good air circulation (prevents mildew)
  • Deadheading for continuous blooms

üêù All About Bees

The Importance of Bees

Bees are among the most important pollinators on Earth. They visit millions of flowers daily, transferring pollen that enables plants to reproduce. Without bees, we would lose many fruits, vegetables, and nuts that depend on pollination.

Plants that attract bees:
